Qeret's economy rests on a foundation of shipbuilding, textiles, and manufacturing, each bolstered by technological innovations drawn from the Ark. The Erutian Harbor and Gate of Wheat handle an unceasing flow of goods; markets like the Nonagon throng with merchants and traders; and the factories along the Bridleway, worked largely by slaves, churn out clothing, tools, and machinery. Maritime industries continue to dominate, but the city's prosperity depends equally on the labor of its citizenry: from the coopers in The Anchor to the merchant magnates of Libnaan’s Bounty, it is the collective industry of its people that sustains Qeret’s growth and influence.

The Workshop and Ark Industry

The Workshop, nestled within the Ark Sector, serves as an Ark factory and primary research facility of the Institute, where innovation moves faster than caution. It produces essential devices such as prompters, Ark tablets, and servitors, supplying much of the city’s basic amenities. Yet it is the Workshop’s spirit of fearless experimentation that defines its character: a crucible where bold research often outpaces the boundaries of convention. Under the permissive stewardship of its senior researcher, Nadaba, students are pushed toward ever more daring pursuits — so long as their work endangers no lives. Though vital to Qeret’s technological ascendance, the Workshop now finds itself at odds with the Sodality’s demand for militarized research and manufacturing, a conflict that continues to strain its founding ethos.
